# All in Security: Why does everyone need to be a 'safety net' in 2026?

**By Pawel Tracichleb, Lead Cybersecurity Assurance with 13 years’ experience in IT**

## Introduction
I’ve seen too many great teams fall for simple phishing just because they thought 'security is someone else’s job'.  
In 2026, we must stop treating security as a department and start treating it as a shared mindset.  
Here's how to implement it in 5 steps.

---

## Step 1: Security by Design
In 2026, we can no longer afford to patch holes after implementation.  
Every new feature or application must have defined security requirements before the first line of code is written.

**Action:** Introduce mandatory Threat Modelling at the sprint planning stage.

---

## Step 2: Every employee is a sensor (Human-Centric Security)
We often say people are the weakest link, but that's a lazy shortcut.  
They are our first line of defense if we give them the right tools.  
Nobody likes year-long compliance training. That’s why I’m a huge fan of these 5-minute 'pills' – they actually stick.

**Action:** Introduce short, 5-minute ‘knowledge pills’ about current threats (e.g., deepfake voice phishing) once a week.

---

## Step 3: Defense automation (AI vs. AI)
In 2026, hackers use AI agents to search for vulnerabilities 24/7.  
Your response must be just as fast.  
The All in Security model involves the use of SOAR tools (e.g., Microsoft Sentinel SOAR capabilities).

**Action:** Automate the blocking of suspicious logins from unusual locations without waiting for an admin decision.

---

## Step 4: Zero Trust principle – no exceptions
We replace ‘trust but verify’ with ‘never trust, always verify.’  
Whether an employee is connecting from the office or a coffee shop, all access to company resources must be authorized using MFA and contextually.

**Action:** Implement systems that verify the status of the device before granting access to the database.

---

## Step 5: Continuous readiness (Incidents are Inevitable)
Assume that an incident will happen.  
All in Security also means having a plan for a quick return to normal operations (Disaster Recovery).

**Action:** Conduct regular ransomware attack simulations so that everyone knows what to do when systems go down.
