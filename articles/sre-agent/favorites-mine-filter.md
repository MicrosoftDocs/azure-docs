# Favorites and Mine filter in Azure SRE Agent

Use **Favorites** and the **Mine** filter to organize your agent's chat sidebar. Star any thread to pin it at the top of your sidebar, and use the **Mine** filter to see only threads you created or favorited.

> [!TIP]
> - Star any thread to pin it in a dedicated **Favorites** section at the top of your sidebar.
> - The **Mine** filter is active by default—your sidebar shows only your threads when you open it.
> - Favorites persist across sessions and are stored per-user in the backend, not just in your browser.
> - Works immediately on any agent requiring no setup or configuration needed.

## The problem: important threads get buried

When your team shares an SRE Agent, the chat sidebar fills up fast. Incident investigations, exploratory queries, scheduled task results, and ad-hoc conversations all compete for space in one chronological list. Finding the thread you were working on an hour ago means scrolling past your teammates' work. During an active incident, the thread you need is always several scrolls down.

Teams work around this problem by naming threads with prefixes like "[Alice] SEV2 storage latency," but that's a manual habit that breaks when autogenerated thread titles take over. There's no built-in way to mark a thread as important or filter the list to just your own work.

## How favorites and Mine filter work

Use the following steps to organize your chat sidebar with favorites and the **Mine** filter.

**1. Star a thread to pin it.** Right-click any thread in the sidebar or open the thread actions menu (**...**) and select **Add to favorites**. The thread moves to a dedicated **Favorites** section at the top of your sidebar.

:::image type="content" source="media/favorites-mine-filter/favorites-context-menu.png" alt-text="Screenshot of thread context menu showing Add to favorites as the first option." lightbox="media/favorites-mine-filter/favorites-context-menu.png":::

**2. Your sidebar splits into two sections.** Once you have favorites, the sidebar shows **Favorites** with your starred threads, and **Chats** with everything else. Both sections scroll independently with infinite loading.

:::image type="content" source="media/favorites-mine-filter/favorites-sidebar-categories.png" alt-text="Screenshot of chat sidebar showing Favorites section at the top and Chats section below." lightbox="media/favorites-mine-filter/favorites-sidebar-categories.png":::

**3. Mine is on by default.** The **Mine** filter is active when you open the sidebar, showing only threads you started in **Chats** and threads you starred in **Favorites**. Toggle it off to see all threads on the agent.

:::image type="content" source="media/favorites-mine-filter/favorites-mine-filter-active.png" alt-text="Screenshot of Mine filter button active with filtered thread list." lightbox="media/favorites-mine-filter/favorites-mine-filter-active.png":::

**4. Combine filters for precision.** **Mine** and **Unread** are independent toggles. Activate both to see only your unread threads across both categories.

**5. Unfavorite when done.** Right-click a favorited thread and select **Remove from favorites** to move it back to the **Chats** section. Keep your **Favorites** list focused on active work.

**6. Favorites persist across sessions.** The backend stores your favorites per user. They survive page refreshes, browser restarts, and device switches. The **Mine** filter is active by default—when you open the sidebar, you see your own threads first. The **Unread** filter resets on page refresh.

## What makes this approach different

Favorites and the **Mine** filter address gaps that browser-based workarounds can't fill.

**Unlike browser bookmarks or sticky notes,** favorites live inside the product. They travel with your account, not your browser tab. Star a thread on your laptop, and it's still starred when you open the portal on a different machine.

**Unlike shared labels or tags,** favorites are personal. Your starred threads are yours—teammates can independently star the same thread without affecting your view. This feature means no "label conflicts" in shared agents.

**Unlike search,** favorites are instant. You don't need to remember a thread title or keyword. Your active investigations are always at the top of the sidebar, one glance away.

## Before and after

The following table summarizes the experience improvements that favorites and the **Mine** filter provide.

|  | Before | After |
|---|---|---|
| **Finding your thread** | Scroll through 20–50 threads in a flat list | Favorites pinned at the top—zero scrolling |
| **Shared agent noise** | Every teammate's threads visible equally | **Mine** filter shows only your work |
| **Marking importance** | Rename threads with naming conventions | Star with one select—no workarounds |
| **Returning after a break** | Hunt for the thread you were working on | Favorites section shows exactly where you left off |
| **Cross-session continuity** | Start fresh every page load | Favorites persist—stored per-user in backend |

## Use cases

The following scenarios show how favorites and the **Mine** filter help in daily operations.

### During an incident

Star the incident thread as soon as you start investigating. As teammates create their own threads and the sidebar grows, your incident is always at the top. After resolution, unstar it.

### Shift handoff

The outgoing SRE stars threads they want the incoming SRE to pick up. The incoming SRE uses **Mine** to focus on just their assigned work.

### Daily monitoring

Star the threads generated by scheduled tasks, such as daily health checks and cost reports, so you can always find and review them easily.
